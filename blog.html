Creating a Personal Website in Bootstrap4

I thought that since I had the basics in CSS, just to complete a personal website assignment, it should've been somewhat easy to use Bootstrap 4. It is, and it isn't. I found out soon enough, that I didn't use CSS anywhere near enough to remember half the things I learned a year ago. Although trying to create something in a short amount of time, Bootstrap 4 was a blessing.

With Bootstrap 4, you have choices to go with a ready-made theme or go with the blank slate basics template, for which you could pick and choose functionality to include for your website, which was what I used. I didn't want to have to go through the confusion of stripping out a bunch of stuff I wasn't going to use.

I wanted something simple and basic. After sketching out something I envisioned, I picked out my Google fonts; then I picked out a simple nav from their gallery. Next, I wanted a plain responsive grid for my projects and blog and simple sticky footer, which gave it a kind of old school throwback to the 90's kind of look. Pretty basic, but using Bootstrap 4, I had something within a day. And from here, I have the freedom to change it any way I want according to any future needs.
